1. Elbow Method

The elbow method evaluates how the inertia (within-cluster variance) changes as the number of clusters (K) increases.

From the plot:

There is a sharp decrease in inertia from K = 2 to K = 4
After K = 4, the curve begins to flatten, indicating diminishing improvement

This point of inflection at K = 4 represents the “elbow,” where adding more clusters does not significantly improve clustering performance.

2. Silhouette Score

The silhouette score measures how well-separated and cohesive the clusters are, with values ranging from -1 to 1.

From the plot:

The highest silhouette score occurs at K = 4 (0.47)
After K = 4, the score declines, indicating poorer cluster separation

This confirms that K = 4 produces the most well-defined clusters
